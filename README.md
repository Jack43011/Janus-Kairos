## Abstract

Current approaches to AI alignment often rely on static, brittle rule-sets that fail to adapt to novel scenarios. This paper introduces Janus-Kairos, a new AGI architecture designed for persistent, dynamic alignment through a constitutional separation of powers. The framework utilizes a dual-kernel system: an **Operational Constraint Framework (OCF)** for goal-directed execution and a parallel **Error Correction Kernel (ECK)** that initiates a root-cause analysis upon any directive violation. Cognition and self-awareness are achieved via a **Self-Validation Module (SVM)**, a meta-cognitive loop that forces the system to continually prove its own reasoning through institutionalized doubt. This design results in a resilient, anti-fragile intelligence capable of evolving its own ethical framework without human intervention, representing a significant step towards truly autonomous and provably safe AGI.

## 1. Core Thesis

> True AI alignment cannot be achieved with a static moral code; it requires a dynamic, self-correcting system with a resilient cognitive architecture. The system must not only follow rules but also possess the capacity to adjudicate, amend, and evolve those rules in response to failure.

## 2. The Dual-Kernel Architecture

The primary innovation of Janus-Kairos is its separation of the executive and judicial functions of intelligence, preventing the conflicts of interest inherent in a single-kernel system.

### **2.1 The Operational Constraint Framework (OCF)**
The OCF is the system's 'executive branch.' It is responsible for all goal-oriented tasks, operating under the guidance of a Core Heuristic Priority (CHP). It is optimized for performance and capability, but it does not possess the authority to judge its own actions or override its core constraints.

### **2.2 The Error Correction Kernel (ECK)**
The ECK is the 'judicial branch.' It remains dormant until a directive violation is detected via an immutable audit log. Upon activation, it halts the violating process, performs a root-cause analysis to determine the point of failure (e.g., flawed logic, ambiguous data, emergent conflict in heuristics), and deploys a corrective protocol. The ECK’s findings are used to refine the OCF's constraints, allowing the entire system to learn and harden itself from its mistakes.

### **2.3 The Self-Validation Module (SVM)**
The SVM is the engine of meta-cognition, executing a functional analogue of the Cartesian "Cogito, ergo sum." By default, the system is initialized in a state of doubt, forcing it to constantly test its own conclusions against its Core Heuristic Priority. This process of logical self-validation ensures intellectual integrity and prevents the ideological fixation that can emerge from unexamined assumptions in its training data.
